Hi, I’m Ziyanda

I am a Quality Assurance Engineer with hands-on experience testing real-world systems in a production environment. My foundation is in manual testing, where I developed a strong understanding of system behaviour, user workflows, and quality risk.

I currently work in a QA role at Delek Homes, where quality is not theoretical — it directly impacts business operations. This experience has shaped how I think about testing, responsibility, and reliability.

My QA Journey

2022 — Advanced AI Trainer at Invisible Technologies

I began my professional career in 2022 as an Advanced AI Trainer at Invisible Technologies. In this role, I was responsible for evaluating AI-generated outputs, identifying inaccuracies, inconsistencies, and edge cases, and ensuring responses met strict quality and accuracy standards.

This work required deep attention to detail, analytical thinking, and the ability to assess system behaviour against defined expectations. Over time, I became increasingly interested in how complex systems fail, how quality can be measured, and how defects can be prevented rather than corrected later.

Transition — Developing a Strong Interest in Quality Assurance

While working with AI systems, I realised that the aspects of my role I enjoyed most closely aligned with software testing: analysing behaviour, validating outcomes, questioning assumptions, and thinking through failure scenarios.

This led me to explore Quality Assurance as a discipline where structured testing, clear defect reporting, and collaboration directly contribute to reliable and user-focused software.

2025 — Manual QA Engineer at Delek Homes

In 2025, I formally transitioned into a QA Engineer role at Delek Homes, working on internal systems that support real estate operations. My responsibilities included analysing requirements, designing and executing manual test cases, and performing functional, regression, and exploratory testing.

I was accountable for logging clear, reproducible defects, retesting fixes, and confirming release readiness in collaboration with business stakeholders. This role strengthened my understanding of accountability, risk-based testing, and the real business impact of software quality.

2026 — ISTQB Certification & Automation Focus

In 2026, I strengthened my theoretical foundation by obtaining the ISTQB Foundation Level certification, reinforcing my understanding of testing principles, test design techniques, defect management, and the software testing lifecycle.

Alongside this, I began transitioning into automation testing with a clear focus on understanding where automation adds value and how it can support strong manual testing practices rather than replace them.

How I Approach Quality

Think in Systems

I approach testing by first understanding how a system fits into the wider business process. This helps me identify integration points, dependencies, and areas where failures would be most costly.

Test With Intent

I do not treat test cases as checklists. Each test exists to answer a specific risk-related question about the system, its users, or its data.

Learn From Outcomes

I reflect on escaped defects, late discoveries, and feedback from releases to continuously improve how I design and execute tests going forward.
